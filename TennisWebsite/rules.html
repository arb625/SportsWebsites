Rules of Tennis

The right to serve, receive, choose your side, or give your opponent these choices is decided by a toss of a coin or racquet. If the choice of service or receiver is chosen, the opponent chooses which side to start. If not, it’s the other way around.
The server is the player who delivers the ball and the receiver is the one who gets it. The server serves the first point from his right-hand side. The server alternates between serving from right and left of the middle of the baseline every point so that all even number points are played on the deuce court (the right) and all odd number points are played on the advantage court (the left). When serving from his right, the server must hit the ball into the square box on the left side of the opposite court and when serving from his left, he must hit it in the right box. If the server commits two faults, which is when he misses the box or hits the net, or if he commits a foot fault by stepping on the baseball before making contact with the ball, he loses the point. If the ball hits the net on a serve and then falls in the correct box, the server is granted another serve. If the receiver hits the ball before it bounces, the server automatically wins the point.
When a point is being played out, if someone hits the ball into the net or if they hit it out of the big rectangle (not including the two alleys in singles and including them in doubles), they lose the point. If the ball hits any part of the outer lines of the rectangle, it is in. If the ball hits the net and goes onto the other side, unless it’s a serve, the ball is in play. A player automatically loses the point if he touches the net during a point.     
The server is responsible for calling out the score. He calls out his score followed by the opponents’ score. At the beginning of every game, each player starts out with zero points, which is called Love. Whichever player wins the first point gets a score of 15. From there it goes to 30, then 40, and then if the player wins another point, he wins the game. However, if the score is tied at 40-40, or deuce, then the player who wins the next points is given advantage and the score becomes Ad-40. The player with advantage must win the next point also to win the game. If he loses it, the score is back to deuce. The player to win six games with a lead of two or more wins the set. If the game count is 6-6, the players play a tiebreaker, where they alternate serves every two points after the first point and the first to 7 points wins the set. A player must win 2 sets out of 3 or 3 out of 5 to win the whole match.
