History of Tennis

The origins of can be traced back to sometime between the 11th and 12th centuries, when French monks started to play a game using their hands and a cork and leather ball where they hit the ball over a piece of rope they tied. This game was called “jeu de paume” which translated to “game of the hand.” This rudimentary game would evolve to one of the most popular sports in the world. Tennis’ popularity took off when nobility adopted the game from the monks. Tennis spread through France very quickly.
The game continued rising in popularity and by the 13th century, improvements and changes were made. The first racquet, a paddle with a handle, was introduced. The game’s popularity took off in England as well. The tennis courts in the 13th century were somewhere in the castle of the royals who played it. The rules were very different and the balls could be hit off walls.
In 1850, Charles Goodyear invented vulcanized rubber which led to the introduction of a bouncy rubber tennis ball. This new kind of tennis ball brought tennis outdoors and it made the sport start resembling modern tennis.
In 1874, Major Walter Wingfield patented the equipment and rules of tennis that are used today. The original court Wingfield designed was in the shape of an hourglass which was not as big as the modern tennis court. Obviously, his version of tennis courts and the rules he created underwent many changes. All these improvements have led to the tennis we play today.
